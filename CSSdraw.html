Debuted in June 1999, this collection in the Serpentine Gallery was brought together by Bridget Riley and 30 of her paintings that were created throughout the 1970's and 1960's. This painting displays numberous vertical lines all varying in width and color with a stand alone black line on the left side of the image. The final design includes colors of blues, pinks, and yellows, as well as offering some black and white lines in the mix as well. This specific design that I recreated here was a section of her original work that was sold in a limited 200 copies, this screenprinted piece on wove paper was sold by the Serpintine Gallery themselves in London. I chose to create this piece beacause I really enjoyed the singular black line that stand out from the rest. The original painting that was created featured multiple black vertical lines, but in this screenprinted version only one is present in the final design.
